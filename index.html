Why this paper

Self-driving cars that see only through their own sensors are blind to what's around the corner. REACT gives the car a second brain: a lightweight Vision–Language–Action model that fuses roadside hazard alerts with the car's own cameras, reasons about the threat in plain language, and outputs the actual steering and acceleration to avoid it. It cuts collisions by 84.9%, sets a new state of the art on the DeepAccident benchmark, and runs in 0.57 seconds on a single NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin, rivaling the safety of models 14× its size at a fraction of the latency.

  1. A reasoner that drives

    A fine-tuned Vision–Language–Action model that goes beyond describing the scene to output the real low-level steering and acceleration the car executes, reasoning step by step about the hazard first.

  2. Residual Trajectory Fusion

    Instead of betting the whole plan on the model, REACT predicts a small discrete correction and fuses it with the car's nominal trajectory, combining accuracy with a built-in safety net.

  3. Real-time on the edge

    Quantization, shorter prompts and parameter-efficient fine-tuning shrink a capable VLA to 0.57 s inference on automotive hardware, with no datacenter required.

  4. Safety that holds up

    84.9% fewer collisions, steady across day and night, four weather types and four road types, not a single cherry-picked scenario.
